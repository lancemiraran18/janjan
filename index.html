SUMMARY OF INCA CIVILIZATION

The Inca were a South American people who ruled an empire along the Pacific coast and Andes Mountains, from northern Ecuador to central Chile. Centered in Cuzco, the empire began expanding under the fourth emperor and reached its peak under Topa Inca Yupanqui and his successor, controlling 12 million subjects by the early 16th century. The Inca built an extensive road system, developed advanced architecture, and maintained a stratified society with an aristocratic bureaucracy and a state religion centered on gods like the sun god. The empire fell to Spanish conquistadors in 1532, who used the Incan roads for their conquest. Today, descendants, primarily Quechua-speaking farmers and herders, live in the Andes, blending Catholicism with traditional beliefs.
